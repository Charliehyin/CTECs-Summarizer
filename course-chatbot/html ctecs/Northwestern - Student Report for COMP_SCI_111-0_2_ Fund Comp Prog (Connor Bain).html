As someone with no programming experience, this class was interesting...basic structure is as follows: on Mondays, there's a lecture previewing the topic of the week and the homework is posted; Wednesdays, you're assigned practice problems related to said topic that you're supposed to work with your group on (you can do it by yourself outside of class, but then it's for accuracy); on Fridays, it's review and required practice (attendance taken). 94 is an A for some reason but there are helpful policies in place (drop lowest quiz grade, lowest exercise grade, etc.) that make it an easy A– at least.

Homework: The difficultly spike after the first exercise is real, and expect to spend upwards of 6 hours per assignment. Start as early as possible on Mondays and try to finish by Wednesday at the latest. After that, it's difficult to get help as office hours become packed. Aside from that, it's not hard to get a good grade on these, but they're very challenging at times.

Peer mentors: A useful system some of the time, but a headache at others. Very likely you'll have to visit office hours at least once per exercise. I recommend going to a couple of different people's office hours towards the begin of the quarter to figure out who you like the most. Some PMs are great and actually want to help you find the answer. Others are condescending, do everything in their power to avoid giving you a hint towards the answer, or simply don't even show up on time (or at all) to their published office hours. Find good times and mentors and stick with them.

Quizzes: The exams in this class are 4 "quizzes": one final, while the other 3 cover topics from that portion of the class. 1st is easy, 2nd is very difficult, and 3rd is somewhere in–between. Biggest problem with these is that they have no resemblance whatsoever with the lectures, exercises, tutorials, or literal practice quizzes that they give you to study with. Functions are 10x longer or more complex than anything you discuss in class, and you only have about 3 min/question to figure out the answer. This makes studying very difficult, but at least you get to drop one.

Language: Yes, Racket is annoying, but people overstate that. However, it does mean the internet can't help you any with problems. It does a good job at teaching the fundamentals of programming, and if you've never programmed before, you might even have a leg up (no other language to confuse it with).
