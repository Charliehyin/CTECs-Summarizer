Class is structured backward: you watch video lectures before class to learn the content and then take a mini–quiz (easy quizzes, act as a grade booster) to prove you did so. And then in class, you work on the weekly exercises or specific assignments covering the topic of that day. While it's nice to have in–class time to do work, motivation to watch the videos before lecture is lacking. Finally, Racket is obviously not a language you're really going to use in the future whatsoever, so while its basic structure is good for learning computer science fundamentals, it gets tedious and honestly annoying that you'll never use it again.
