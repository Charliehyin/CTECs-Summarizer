PROS
– Joe Hummel is the best!!! Best CS professor I've had so far. He is extremely knowledgable about the content and is a very caring and dedicated instructor. He answered my questions patiently and very insightfully, and he is very responsive on Piazza.
– Contrary to what some people may claim, this class is actually very easy to do well in. If you put in the effort to work on the program methodically, and go to office hours if you need help (Hummel basically walks you through the solution), there is no reason why you shouldn't get 100% in every project. About 80–90% of all students eventually get a 100% on the project, so that tells you that doing well is entirely possible. People mainly complain because projects take a long time (more on that below), but this isn't CS 111 or 150 anymore so you just need to get used to it. If you struggle in this class, just take it again in a lighter quarter.
– Grading is very reasonable. You can run your code an unlimited number of times on Gradescope (except for the last project), late policy is very lenient (2% deducted every 12 hours) and the final exam was very fair and similar to the practice final.
– Lectures are engaging, which is partly because Hummel is a great guy with good vibes that you just want to listen to him, and partly thanks to the in–class coding exercises that give you some hands on experience with the C and C++ concepts covered.

CONS
– The first few projects in C were a lot of work, even for someone with C experience. Weekly projects took anywhere from 4 to 16 hours (24 for some of my friends.
